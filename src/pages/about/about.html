What is the Local Loop Application?

The Local Loop System is a fun way to reflect your spending, more specifically your local spernding. At the same time it will help us all to understand better how money circulates around the local economy. To do this we need to collect some information about you and your spending.

swipe >> for what information do you need?

What Information Do You Need?

In order to understand the money being sepnt and where we need to have certain pieces of information. We have to know where you live (a postcode) and where you shop (the spend). We use this information to calculate if you spent locally and if the people you spend with also spend locally etc. This way we track the money. If the money returns to you by some means that is a 'perfect loop'.

What About my Personal Data?

We care very deeply about your data and about your privacy. We ask your name/email for login/help and to log your spend/points. Your age band helps us to understand how spending changes between generations. We need to know where you live to understand what is local. We ask you to put in your income and if you are employed locally to understand how local spend affects you and to make loops. We don't collect gender or other personal details and we will never share your details except with you.

What Data is Shared?

We believe that the data on local spending (how it makes better local economies) will help organisations, individulas, government and industry invest and plan for the local communities. We therefore wish to share this data with them freely. We will anonymise the data (remove the personal details) as they will never need to be shared. So we will share the loops and how the money is spent and re-spent in a local area and statistical analysis based upon this record.

What is the Local Loop Game?

How much does your money actually make and why is it important are questions that are increasingly relevant to many people. We have decided to collect the data and examine how the money circulates around our local economy. We have also added some fun by awarding you points when you make a local spend, or a company you spend with uses local suppliers and contributes to the local economy.

swipe >> for how are points calculated?

How are Points Calculated?

Points are calculated on the value of your spend and the subsequent re-spend. When the receipt you submit is “local” (defined by distance to your postcode), you are awarded points. More points are awarded when the money you have spent is re-spent by the business you spent with. So local shopping for you and the companies you spend with are always rewarded.

swipe << What is the Local Loop Game

What is Local?

Local is variable distance with some mathematics and common sense applied. For some goods and services, food, for instance, local might be a 20-30 mile distance from your home address. However for a digital technology company, local might be the town where you live. The key thing we measure is how things are local relative to you. And what we are looking for is for spending that stays in the local area and which, ultimately, in the case of your own spending, enhances the local economy and therefore gives return to you.

What is the Value of Local?

People say shopping local is best, but what is the proof? We can claim that a pound spent with a local shop that is re-spent with a local supplier who employs local staff is enhancing the local economy, but can we prove that? By collecting this data and seeing where the money goes we can look at how your spending affects local businesses, how you make a direct difference and how that is returned to you.

What Are Local Loops?

A local loop is money that travels around the local economy. You spend with local shops, who buys from local suppliers, who employs local people. The money doesn't leave the system it loops around it.

swipe >> for local multiplier

The Local Multiplier

The New Economics Foundation defined a Local Loop Multiplier which inspired the creation of the Local Loop system. This system is intended to further quantify local spending. It intends to go further than the Leaky Bucket Metaphor which is used by NEF to signify data that can no longer be tracked or has left the local economy.

The application is intended to operate by the principles of the Quadruple Bottom Line as defined by the Small Green Consultancy and used by the Ethical Small Traders Association.
